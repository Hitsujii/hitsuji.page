Hello!!

I made this blog so I could write about learning C++.

_That was the idea, at least._

The plan was simple: set up a small site, write a quick first post, and then go back to the thing I actually wanted to do: write some `.cpp`.

Unfortunately :( I gave the computer too much room to suggest side quests.

<TOCInline toc={props.toc} exclude="Introduction" />

## The problem

I opened [learncpp.com](https://www.learncpp.com/) for the first time.
That was supposed to be the beginning, but before I wrote any meaningful code,
somehow, learncpp.com became a React course.

_wtf?_

I do not fully know how that happened.
I just noticed one small thing to fix on the blog. Then another. And another. And I kept telling myself this was still part of learning.

_To be fair, it kind of was._

## Getting ready

Getting ready to learn can look a lot like learning.

You make the folder. You pick the theme. You organize the notes.
You improve the website. You make everything feel nice and clean and ready.
Then you _(I)_ realize you _(I)_ wasted the whole evening preparing to start.

**It's dangerous because it feels productive.**

It is not doomscrolling. It is not obviously useless. It has buttons, files, commits, and maybe even a README. So it becomes very easy to lie to yourself _(myself)_.

You are preparing.

_I'm preparing._

## The detour

Choosing a simple Astro template and moving on with my life?

_lmao._

I wanted the lightness of Astro, especially that one certain template, but with the guts of Next.js.
Idk why, but the idea of being limited by `.astro` was too scary for me to actually use it.

_Very reasonable, even though I would probably never even get close to using the actual advantages of Next.js._

So I started tweaking things. Then I tweaked the things around the things. In the end that detour turned into [NextPaper](https://github.com/Hitsujii/next-paper),
a Next.js blog starter inspired by this pretty [AstroPaper](https://github.com/satnaing/astro-paper) template and built on top of [Tailwind Next.js Starter Blog](https://github.com/timlrx/tailwind-nextjs-starter-blog) just to make it faster.

Was this necessary for learning C++? No.

Was it fun? Yes.

Do I regret it? Not really.

## So what now?

Now the blog exists, which means I can finally use it for the thing it was supposed to be for.

_right?_

I want to keep this place honest. No pretending to know things.
No skipping the boring parts. No collecting syntax and hoping understanding magically appears later.
Just learning, writing, making mistakes, fixing them, and writing down what happened.

_So yeah, fuck AI._

Out of all this getting ready to get ready, I somehow got something useful.
A finished project. A topic for the first post. Proof that I was doing the thing, even if I was doing it badly.

And mostly, a warning for myself.

The setup is done now. Probably. Hopefully.

Now I should probably write some `.cpp`.

Thank you for reading!

**This is my first post, and the first mistake I am documenting.**
